    Under section 107 of the Copyright Act of 1976, allowance is made for "fair use" and "transformative use" for purposes such as teaching, education and research. Fair use is a use permitted by copyright statute that might otherwise be infringing. Transformative uses are those that add something new, with a further purpose or different character, and do not substitute for the original use of the work. We believe that copyrighted items used in this web site abide by the US Copyright Act in being fair and transformative in an educational vein.

    Fair Use Definition

    Fair use is a doctrine in United States copyright law that allows limited use of copyrighted information without requiring permission from the rights holders, such as commentary, criticism, news reporting, research, teaching or scholarship. It provides for the legal, non-licensed citation or incorporation or copyrighted material in another author's work under a four-factor balancing test.
