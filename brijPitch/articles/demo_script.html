    The Skinny

    The easiest way to explain the capabilities of Brij is to detail a Case Study. We intend to use this case study to build a WalkThrough with the finished product. We believe that this demo (a video) will be sufficient to showcase the product features and capabilities to potential purchasers.

    The case study is a fictional project Mr. Dithers hands to Dagwood in a comic strip with three scenes. The episode ends with the question: How did Dagwood complete the project (the answer is: with Brij!) Switch to a screencast showing Dagwood using Brij to:

    • Create a new Table
    • Import 3M records
    • Add fields
    • Create a Form
    • Create and configure five Queries

    The Details: A Case Study

    Act I Scene I: Dagwood's latest Project


    Fair Use Notice

    It is just after Thanksgiving, and senior management is either running annual figures or planning for the next year. Mr. Dithers walks into Dagwood's office.

    "Dagwood, I just emailed you a data file," he says. "I asked the IT dept. to generate a dump of all the fields I need for a new project. Let me tell you about the project. We're thinking of introducing a new Paint line - the SilkenTouch - which many customers are very excited about. It gives really high-quality finishes but is much cheaper than Emulsion-based paints.

    Dagwood nods. Mr. Dithers continues.

    "I've had Annie (the Customer Relations manager) to get her people to end every customer call with a survey question about their willingness to be part of a Pilot Paint Project. They were asked to rate their interest on a scale from 1 to 10, and the customer reps have added that information to the Customer Details field, on a new line, in the following format: {Pilot:##}."

    Dagwood nods. Mr. Dithers continues.

    "The data dump should be about 3 Million records. Sometime tommorrow I'll email you a list of the parameters I need. For instance, we don't want data on any Chain Hardware Stores - except for the West Zone."

    Dagwood nods. Mr. Dithers continues.

    "I need the data filtered, sorted and grouped in different ways to help me hand-select the pilot group. I don't really need to add any data except for my ranking of suitability to be included in the Pilot Project. I'll also send you a list of all the screens I need tommorrow."

    Dagwood nods. Mr. Dithers continues.

    "I don't care what software you use or how you do this, it's internal use only. Let me know if you have any questions."

    The next day Dagwood opens an email with the following attachment from Mr. Dithers

    The email also contains a 3M record CSV file, and Mr. Dithers explains that:

    • Some records have the text "{Pilot:XX}" in the CustomerDetails field. He only wants clients with 5 or more in this field (indicating level of interest to participate in the Pilot Project)
    • He wants the West Zone excluded
    • He wants no Chain Stores except "Ace Hardware" (a special relationship with that client)
    • He needs the following five screens 1. a 2. b 3. c 4. d 5. A SelectedClients Screen showing all clients selected so far (marked Selected by Mr. Dithers).
    • From any of these screens he needs to be able to Select a client for the Pilot. Selecting the client should automatically show the client in the SelectedClients Screen

    Act I Scene II: Two weeks later

    Mr. Dithers: "Dagwood, I forgot to tell you: I don't care whether you use a mix of internal and external developers; and I don't care whether you get a few senior guys or mostly junior people, BUT - and this is important - keep the budget under a hundred thousand for the entire project. Also, try to get it done by the end of January."

    Dagwood: "Mr. Dithers, the application will be ready next week."

    Mr. Dithers: "WHAT?! How many guys did you hire?"

    Dagwood: "None, I did it myself."
